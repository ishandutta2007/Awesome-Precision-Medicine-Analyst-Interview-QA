# Resources: Further Reading & Tools for Precision Medicine Analysts

## Key Guidelines & Standards
- **ACMG/AMP Standards and Guidelines for the Interpretation of Sequence Variants** (Richards et al., 2015, Genetics in Medicine) — foundational variant classification framework
- **ACMG SF v3.2 gene list** — secondary/incidental findings recommendations
- **CPIC Guidelines** (cpicpgx.org) — drug-gene dosing guidelines, continuously updated
- **TRIPOD Statement** — reporting guideline for prediction model studies
- **GA4GH Standards** (ga4gh.org) — Global Alliance for Genomics and Health interoperability standards

## Textbooks
- **"Genomic and Precision Medicine"** (Ginsburg & Willard, eds.) — comprehensive multi-volume reference
- **"Clinical Genomics: A Guide to Clinical Next Generation Sequencing"** (Kulkarni & Pfeifer) — practical NGS interpretation
- **"Modern Epidemiology"** (Rothman, Greenland, Lash) — causal inference and confounding foundations
- **"Survival Analysis: A Self-Learning Text"** (Kleinbaum & Klein) — accessible biostatistics reference

## Landmark Papers
- Richards et al. (2015) — ACMG/AMP variant classification standards
- Relling & Klein (2011) — CPIC founding paper
- Manolio et al. (2013) — Implementing genomic medicine in clinical practice
- Martin et al. (2019) — Clinical use of current polygenic risk scores may exacerbate health disparities (Nature Genetics)
- Sherman et al. (2019) — Genomic reference sample diversity gaps
