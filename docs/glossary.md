# Glossary: Precision Medicine Terminology

## Terms A–M

**ACMG/AMP** – American College of Medical Genetics and Genomics / Association for Molecular Pathology; body publishing variant classification guidelines.

**ACMG SF list** – Secondary findings gene list; genes recommended for reporting incidental pathogenic findings regardless of testing indication.

**Allele frequency** – Proportion of a specific allele among all alleles at a locus in a population; key evidence for variant classification.

**BAM** – Binary Alignment Map; compressed file format storing aligned sequencing reads.

**Basket trial** – Clinical trial testing one targeted therapy across multiple tumor types sharing a common biomarker.

**Bonferroni correction** – Conservative multiple-testing correction dividing significance threshold by number of tests.

**CDx (Companion Diagnostic)** – Test required for safe/effective use of a corresponding therapeutic.

**CDS (Clinical Decision Support)** – Systems providing clinicians with patient-specific guidance at point of care.

**CLIA** – Clinical Laboratory Improvement Amendments; US federal quality standards for clinical labs.

**ClinVar** – Public archive of variant-phenotype relationships with clinical significance assertions.

**CNV (Copy Number Variant)** – Structural variant involving duplication or deletion of genomic segments.

**Companion diagnostic** – See CDx.

**Confounding by indication** – Bias where the reason for treatment is itself associated with outcome.

**CPIC** – Clinical Pharmacogenetics Implementation Consortium; publishes drug-gene dosing guidelines.

**CRAM** – Reference-based compressed alignment format, smaller than BAM.

**ctDNA** – Circulating tumor DNA; cell-free DNA shed by tumors, detectable in blood (liquid biopsy).

**De Novo classification** – FDA pathway for novel low/moderate-risk devices without existing predicates.

**Diplotype** – Combination of two haplotypes/alleles at a locus (e.g., pharmacogenomic star-allele pair).

**Enrichment trial design** – Trial enrolling only biomarker-positive patients.

**FDR (False Discovery Rate)** – Expected proportion of false positives among rejected null hypotheses; Benjamini-Hochberg correction.

**FHIR** – Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources; standard for healthcare data exchange.

**Founder variant** – Pathogenic variant enriched in a population due to shared ancestry/genetic bottleneck.

**GDPR** – General Data Protection Regulation; EU data privacy law treating genetic data as a special category.

**GINA** – Genetic Information Nondiscrimination Act; US law prohibiting genetic discrimination in employment/health insurance (not life/disability insurance).

**gnomAD** – Genome Aggregation Database; large population allele frequency reference.

**GVCF** – Genomic VCF; per-sample intermediate file enabling joint genotyping across cohorts.

**GWAS** – Genome-Wide Association Study; tests genetic variants across the genome for phenotype association.

**Haplotype** – Set of DNA variants inherited together on the same chromosome.

**HGVS** – Human Genome Variation Society nomenclature for describing variants.

**HL7** – Health Level Seven; healthcare data exchange standards organization.

**HLA** – Human Leukocyte Antigen; highly polymorphic genes relevant to drug hypersensitivity and transplant matching.

**Incidental finding** – Unexpected clinically significant result unrelated to the original testing indication.

**Instrumental variable** – Statistical technique approximating randomization in observational causal inference.

**LDT (Laboratory Developed Test)** – In-house diagnostic test developed and used within a single laboratory.

**LOINC** – Logical Observation Identifiers Names and Codes; standard for lab test identification.

**Master protocol** – Overarching trial infrastructure (umbrella/basket/platform) testing multiple hypotheses.

**MRD (Minimal Residual Disease)** – Low-level residual cancer cells detectable after treatment; used as outcome surrogate.

**Multi-omics** – Integration of genomics, transcriptomics, proteomics, metabolomics, and other molecular data layers.

## Terms N–Z

**NGS** – Next-Generation Sequencing; high-throughput sequencing technologies.

**OMOP CDM** – Observational Medical Outcomes Partnership Common Data Model; standardized schema for multi-site health data.

**Penetrance** – Proportion of individuals with a genotype who express the associated phenotype.

**Pharmacodynamic biomarker** – Marker measuring biological response confirming drug target engagement.

**Pharmacogenomics (PGx)** – Study of how genetic variation affects drug response.

**Phenoconversion** – Drug-drug interaction causing a patient's phenotype to differ from their genotype-predicted metabolizer status.

**PMA (Premarket Approval)** – Most rigorous FDA pathway, required for high-risk (Class III) devices.

**Polygenic Risk Score (PRS)** – Aggregate score summarizing genome-wide variant effects on disease risk.

**Predictive biomarker** – Marker predicting differential treatment response.

**Prognostic biomarker** – Marker predicting outcome regardless of treatment.

**Propensity score** – Modeled probability of treatment assignment given covariates; used to reduce confounding in observational studies.

**Proportional hazards assumption** – Cox model assumption that hazard ratios are constant over time.

**Reference genome** – Composite representative genome sequence used for alignment (e.g., GRCh38).

**RWD (Real-World Data)** – Patient health data collected outside traditional RCTs.

**RWE (Real-World Evidence)** – Clinical evidence derived from analysis of RWD.

**SNOMED CT** – Comprehensive clinical terminology standard.

**Star allele** – Pharmacogenomic haplotype nomenclature (e.g., CYP2D6*4).

**Structural variant (SV)** – Large-scale genomic alteration (deletion, duplication, inversion, translocation).

**Surrogate endpoint** – Outcome measure substituting for a true clinical endpoint (e.g., MRD for survival).

**TRIPOD** – Transparent Reporting of a multivariable prediction model for Individual Prognosis Or Diagnosis; reporting guideline.

**Umbrella trial** – Trial testing multiple targeted therapies each matched to biomarker-defined subgroups within one disease.

**VCF (Variant Call Format)** – Standard text file format for storing genetic variant data.

**VUS (Variant of Uncertain Significance)** – Variant with insufficient evidence for pathogenic/benign classification.

**WES** – Whole Exome Sequencing.

**WGS** – Whole Genome Sequencing.
